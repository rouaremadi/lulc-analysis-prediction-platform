Drivers of Projected Change
🏗
Urban Sprawl & Demographic Pressure
Continued population growth in Greater Tunis drives residential and industrial expansion deep into Manouba's periurban zone, consuming agricultural and bare land at a projected rate of 5–8 km²/yr through 2040.
🌾
Agricultural Land Abandonment
Rural-urban migration reduces farming intensity. Olive groves and cereal plots, when left unmanaged, shift toward bare soil or are annexed by informal construction, accelerating the vegetation decline signal.
💧
Hydrological Regime Shifts
Upstream Sidi Salem dam regulation and increasing irrigation extraction shrink Medjerda river-adjacent water bodies. Climate projections for North Africa indicate −10 to −20% annual rainfall by 2040.
🏜
Soil Sealing & Erosion
Impermeable surface expansion accelerates runoff, reducing infiltration and topsoil regeneration. Combined with accelerated erosion on devegetated slopes, bare soil patches are projected to persist and expand.
🏭
Infrastructure Corridors
Planned highway upgrades and the Tunis logistics hub development will permanently seal an estimated 12–18 km² of currently vegetated or agricultural land between 2025 and 2040.
🌡
Climate Change Amplification
CMIP6 projections for North Africa show +1.5–2.5°C warming by 2040 relative to 2000 baseline, extending dry season length and intensifying heat stress on existing vegetation cover in low-elevation zones.
05 — Study Information

About the Project

This project documents 40 years of Land Use and Land Cover change in the Manouba governorate of Tunisia, using multi-temporal satellite imagery classified into four primary land cover classes: Urban, Vegetation, Water, and Bare Soil.

The study period from 1985 to 2025 spans key socioeconomic transitions — from agrarian land use patterns prior to liberalization, through to rapid suburban expansion driven by Greater Tunis growth pressure.

Classification was performed on Landsat 5 TM, Landsat 7 ETM+, Landsat 8 OLI, and Sentinel-2 MSI imagery using supervised classification algorithms. Change detection layers record transitions between epochs and are distributed as GeoJSON.

Future projections leverage Markov chain modeling and cellular automaton simulation, calibrated on observed land cover transition matrices derived from the 1985–2025 classification series, projecting scenarios to 2040.

Markov model calibrated on 1985–2025 matrices; 2030–2040 projections
